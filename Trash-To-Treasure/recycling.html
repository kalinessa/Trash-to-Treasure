Benefits of Recycling

  • Reduces waste that would end up in incinerators.
  • Conserves resources and energy. For example, energy saved from recycling 10 plastic bottles can power a laptop for 25 hours (EPA).
  • Reduces the need to extract new materials, which reduces greenhouse gas emissions.

Guidelines

Check your local recycling regulations to see what items may or may not be recycled. View the general categories below:

  • Paper: newspapers, magazines, books, office paper without wax liners
  • Cardboard: flattened cereal boxes, packing boxes
  • Cans: steel and aluminum cans
  • Cartons: juice and milk cartons
  • Bottles: plastic and glass bottles
  • Plastic bags
  • Certain plastic cups and containers
  • Plastic caps or lids
  • Styrofoam
  • Bubble wrap
  • Paper or cardboard with food waste, such as pizza boxes
  • Chip bags and wrappers
  • Batteries
  • Flatten all cardboard boxes before recycling. If there are food scraps or grease on the cardboard, most likely it should not be recycled.
  • Some places may require removing certain elements in order to recycle an item. For example, remove caps and labels from bottles before recycling, and remove the eraser and metal part from a wooden pencil.
  • Ensure that food and beverage containers are clean and empty before recycling. There should be no food scraps, which you can compost instead.
  • Plastic bags may get caught in recycling equipment if collected from curbside recycling, disrupting the process. You can instead reuse plastic bags, and some grocery stores and retailers collect plastic bags to recycle.
