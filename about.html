What is Trash to Treasure (T3)?

Trash to Treasure, or T3, was founded by four students who hope to raise awareness about recycling and reducing waste. T3 uses artificial intelligence, trained on hundreds of images, to quickly help you determine whether an object is recyclable or not.

Why Did We Create T3?

The US produced over 292 million tons of trash in 2018, only 32% of which was recycled or composted (EPA). According to a 2019 report by The Tishman Environment and Design Center at The New School, 79% of trash incinerators fall within communities that are low income and predominantly people of color (Baptista & Perovich, 2019). These sites expose communities to pollution, posing health concerns. More than any other state, Florida has 10 trash incinerators. Waste is an environmental justice issue that is closely tied to climate change and discrimination.

Recycling is often considered a solution to waste. However, recycling is complicated: Guidelines about what to recycle vary by location. Contamination occurs when people mistakenly put non-recyclable items into recycling bins, which interferes with the recycling process and damages equipment. According to Recyclops, around 19-40% of collected recyclables are contaminated. Additionally, many recyclable items fail to be properly recycled. In 2018, only 9% of plastics, 25% of glass, and 68% of paper was recycled or composted (EPA).

Contamination is a prominent reason why Davie, the town where the T3 team attend school, has stopped recycling in recent years. Instead, all recyclables are sent to an incinerator, thrown away like trash. Other cities around the US are doing the same. Our team built the Trash to Treasure AI project to raise awareness about this issue and educate people about what can and cannot be recycled.
